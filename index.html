About

Stewardship at the Scale of the Modern Research University

Manish Kumar has spent more than twenty years inside the financial and administrative engine rooms of America's public research universities, advising presidents and governing boards on the questions that determine whether an institution thrives or merely survives. His portfolio at Bowie State University — finance, budget, treasury, human resources, procurement, facilities, IT, and capital planning — reflects the breadth of responsibility that defines the modern higher education CFO.

Before Bowie State, Mr. Kumar held senior financial leadership roles at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Rutgers University, and Northeastern Illinois University. His tenure at NEIU is widely cited as a case study in financial turnaround: a four-notch Moody's credit rating upgrade, more than $43 million in supplemental state and federal funding secured, and a return to institutional stability that opened space for academic reinvestment.

What unites the work across institutions is a conviction that financial strategy must serve academic mission — not the other way around. That belief shows up in his approach to enterprise modernization, governance, and shared decision-making, and in the partnerships he builds with faculty, staff, and external stakeholders.
